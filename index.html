The Problem

ERP decisions are made on gut feel.
The real cost stays invisible.

Most organizations don't upgrade their ERP because they can't see the cost of not upgrading. They feel the friction but they can't quantify it.

Labor Leakage

Workarounds become the workflow

Reconciliations, shadow spreadsheets, manual re-keying, and approvals outside the system pile up silently each one burning hours every week.

Working Capital

Excess inventory sits on the balance sheet

When planners don't trust system data, they over-order. That capital has a cost but it rarely appears in any ERP business case.

Decision Friction

The same data environment that creates cost exposure also delays its resolution. Friction compounds and compounds quietly.

The Framework

Three pillars. One clear number.

OCEI structures the diagnostic across three evidence-based pillars, each calculated conservatively and independently verifiable.

01
Pillar One

Labor Leakage from Manual Workarounds

Quantifies recurring staff time spent on system workarounds reconciliations, offline approvals, re-keying, shadow reporting converted to annual cost using a fully-loaded rate (including taxes, benefits, and overhead).

02
Pillar Two

Inventory Cash Exposure from Planning Distrust

Calculates the annual carrying cost of excess inventory held because planners don't trust system outputs. Based on average inventory value, a diagnostic-score-derived excess band, and a standard carrying cost rate.

03
Pillar Three

Decision Friction Multiplier

A persistence multiplier applied to Pillars 1 and 2, reflecting how the same data environment that creates exposure also delays its resolution. External friction indicators are weighted more heavily.

Process

A structured 60–90 minute discovery session with your operations and finance leads. No consultants. No guesswork.

1

Checklist Walkthrough

Systematically identify workarounds, inventory planning behaviours, and decision friction indicators across your team.

2

Input Collection

3

Exposure Calculation

All three pillars are calculated conservatively. Every assumption is visible and adjustable no black box.

4

Decision Gate

A three-gate framework determines whether the exposure justifies a process-level fix or a full system change.

Decision Gate Framework
Gate 1 Materiality
Is the exposure significant?
Annual cost vs. upgrade investment payback threshold
Gate 2 Persistence

Gate 3 Systemic Root

Distinguishes process failure from system failure
